Before State — Documented Evidence

Six Android Failure Categories, All Sourced

Every quote below is verbatim from Google Play or Quinyx's own support documentation. These are not edge cases — they are patterns appearing across multiple users, multiple OEM families, and multiple Quinyx app versions.

1
Session Drop & Auth Failure — Token Expiry Loop
~50% of reported issues  ·  Highest business impact
★★☆☆☆   Google Play
"Suddenly logs me out for no reason, which can put my job time keeping at high risk as it also sometimes refuses to log me back in due to the enter button not working."
Google Play Review — Quinyx Workforce Management (Android)
⚙
Root cause: OEM battery optimization (Samsung One UI, Huawei EMUI, Xiaomi MIUI) kills background token refresh process → OAuth token expires → re-auth UI freezes. Affects background process scheduling in Android < API 33 without foreground service exemption.
Worker cannot clock in → missed punch → payroll error → labor law exposure (Germany ArbZG, UK Working Time Regulations)
2
Geofencing / GPS Clock-In Blocked
Quinyx-acknowledged limitation
📄
From Quinyx's Own Support Documentation
"The technology has its limitations, and having a short range dramatically impacts the possibility to punch in or out quickly as the technology is not accurate enough to pin-point your location to within a small area."
source: support.quinyx.com — GPS / Geofencing FAQ
⚙
Android-specific: Doze mode kills GPS listener → "not in range" even when worker is at site. Google Issue Tracker #218335535. iOS handles background location differently via CLLocationManager persistent callbacks.
German cluster active: Dachau, Munich, Waiblingen reports Oct 2025–Feb 2026
3
Shift State Corruption After Break Clock-Out
Recurring, manager-escalation required
★☆☆☆☆   Google Play
"A known and ongoing issue: if lunch is after 6pm and you clock out for break, your shift disappears and you can't clock back in."
Google Play Review — described as "known and ongoing"
⚙
Pattern: Break punch-out triggers shift-state write; edge-case (late lunch >18:00) corrupts shift record. Manager must manually reinstate → admin overhead + payroll gap.
Quinyx Time Clock promises "accurate time entries" — this bug directly contradicts that guarantee
4
App Freeze on Launch (Cache Corruption)
Progressive failure — requires full reinstall
★☆☆☆☆   Google Play
"Works fine for a number of weeks then freezes at the Quinyx screen, requiring users to delete and reinstall the app."
Google Play Review — multiple users, same failure arc
⚙
Pattern: Progressive SQLite / Room DB cache corruption → hang on launch screen. Frontline workers on managed device profiles often cannot self-reinstall corporate apps → IT ticket required.
Each reinstall = lost session, lost device trust, IT overhead — invisible cost at 500K+ install scale
5
Messenger Module Broken on Android
Affects Quinyx's 2024 strategic launch
★★☆☆☆   Google Play
"Can't send Qmail to colleagues — the tab for adding people only shows themselves."
Google Play Review — Messenger / Qmail feature
⚙
Context: Quinyx launched Messenger Plus in 2024 as a strategic frontline communication expansion. Android is the primary delivery platform for frontline workers. If Qmail recipient lookup is broken on Android, Messenger Plus is broken where it matters most.
Messenger Plus is a new product — broken Android delivery undermines the strategic bet
6
Post-Update Feature Regressions
v3.43 (Mar 2025) → v3.52 (Jan 2026)
★★☆☆☆   Google Play
"No longer seeing the time tab after recent updates."
Google Play Review — post-update regression, Time tab
⚙
Pattern: 9 versions shipped in 10 months (v3.43 → v3.52). Rapid feature velocity without Android regression safety net. Core navigation elements (Time tab) disappearing post-update indicates missing integration test coverage for Android.
Each regression ships to 500K+ Android users before it is caught — no automated rollback gate
Why This Matters Now

Three Compounding Pressures

These six failure categories do not exist in isolation. They sit at the intersection of three external pressures that make Android reliability a board-level risk in 2026 — not a backlog item.

🏆
IDC MarketScape 2026 — Leader Designation
In January 2026, IDC named Quinyx a Leader in Workforce Management, citing "typically in excess of 90% app engagement among frontline employees" as a key differentiator.
The tension: Quinyx is publicly credited for 90%+ frontline engagement while Android has documented auth failures that prevent workers from logging in at all. These two things cannot both be true at scale.
💬
Messenger Plus — Android Is the Platform
Quinyx launched Messenger Plus in 2024 as its frontline communication layer — a direct expansion of the product surface area onto Android devices. Navigate 2025 declared "mobile-first design" a strategic company priority.
Betting the frontline comms roadmap on Android while Android messaging (Qmail) is provably broken is an internal contradiction that compounds with every feature shipped.
🏳
DACH Expansion — Active Failures Right Now
The German user cluster is the most active failure surface in the current data. Review timestamps from Dachau, Munich, and Waiblingen show a concentrated pattern of auth failures and GPS punch-in failures from October 2025 through February 2026.
German labor law (ArbZG) requires accurate time recording. A failed clock-in is not a UX inconvenience — it is a compliance gap with legal exposure for Quinyx's enterprise customers.
