Four iOS-specific problems that don't solve themselves

On-Device Inference: CoreML vs. TFLite / ONNX

Your Android inference path runs on TFLite or ONNX Runtime Mobile, tuned for Snapdragon 6xx/4xx NPUs. iOS's Neural Engine speaks CoreML exclusively — Apple intentionally walls off direct GPU/ANE access. Quantization work, model conversion, and latency profiling on Android does not transfer. You are looking at a second optimization track: .mlpackage conversion, MLModel configuration flags, compute unit selection (CPU_AND_NE vs. ALL), and per-device latency regression testing from iPhone 13 mini to iPhone 16 Pro Max.

CoreML ANE INT4 quantization coremltools MLComputeUnits
🎤

iOS Background Audio: AVAudioSession Is Not Forgiving

Android's audio architecture is relatively permissive for background recording. iOS is not. You need AVAudioSessionCategoryRecord with correct options, the audio background mode entitlement declared in your Info.plist, and airtight interruption handling via AVAudioSessionInterruptionNotification. When a phone call, system alert, or another app interrupts the session mid-recording, you must deactivate cleanly and resume without data loss. Behavior changed across iOS 15, 16, 17, and 18. A nurse cannot lose a care record because your app did not handle an incoming Facetime call correctly.

HIPAA is Not GDPR with Different Paperwork

Your GDPR architecture — on-device processing, no cloud audio, data minimization — gets you closer to HIPAA than most, but not there. The offline voice queue sitting in local storage is ePHI the moment it enters the app. That means AES-256 encryption at rest with a device-tied key, automatic session timeout enforced at the app layer (not OS lock screen), remote wipe via MDM enrollment, and audit logs capturing every ePHI access event retained for six years. HIPAA's Security Rule §164.312(a)(1) specifies technical access controls the iOS Secure Enclave helps with but does not cover on its own. A Business Associate Agreement will be required with every hospital. None of this is speculative — it is the compliance delta between your current German stack and your US target market.
